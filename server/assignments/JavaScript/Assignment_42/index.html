Sisyphus, a cunning king condemned to the eternal repetition of a meaningless task, embodies the philosophical dilemma of human existence. Doomed to endlessly push a heavy boulder up a hill, only for it to roll back down, he symbolizes the absurdity of striving for goals that ultimately lead to nothing. His plight raises profound questions about the nature of labor, the pursuit of meaning, and the cyclical nature of life and death. The myth of Sisyphus serves as a poignant reminder of the human condition, highlighting the inherent futility of existence and the constant struggle against entropy.
