Kafka's "The Metamorphosis" serves as a poignant allegory for the human condition, exploring the depths of alienation and the loss of individuality.

Gregor's transformation into an insect symbolizes the dehumanizing forces of societal pressures and expectations. His isolation and suffering highlight the fragility of the human spirit and the destructive power of conformity.

The novella invites us to question the nature of identity, the meaning of existence, and the consequences of sacrificing one's true self for the sake of societal acceptance.
